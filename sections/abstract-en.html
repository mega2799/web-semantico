Abstract

The Beer Recipe Ontology is a comprehensive semantic model designed to represent the domain of beer brewing recipes using OWL 2 and linked data principles. This ontology captures the complex relationships between ingredients (malts, hops, yeasts, cereals, water), brewing processes, equipment requirements, and beer styles.

The ontology enables:

  • Recipe Discovery: Query recipes by style, ingredients, or difficulty level
  • Ingredient Substitution: Find alternative ingredients with similar flavor profiles
  • Automated Classification: Infer beer styles based on recipe characteristics (ABV, IBU, EBC)
  • Process Modeling: Represent sequential brewing steps with precise parameters

This work integrates external vocabularies including hops ontology, malt ontology, and FOODON to leverage existing brewing knowledge and ensure semantic interoperability. The ontology follows W3C best practices and includes comprehensive documentation, examples, and SPARQL query patterns.
